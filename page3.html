Atomic Nucleus vs Cell Nucleus

Understanding their meaning, history, and differences

Meaning and Structure

Atomic Nucleus

The atomic nucleus is the extremely dense center of an atom.

It contains protons and neutrons and holds almost all the mass of the atom. The particles are held together by the strong nuclear force.

Cell Nucleus

The cell nucleus is an organelle found inside eukaryotic cells.

It stores DNA and controls activities such as cell growth, protein production, and reproduction.

History of Discovery

1831 – Robert Brown discovered the nucleus inside plant cells while studying orchids under a microscope.
1911 – Ernest Rutherford discovered the atomic nucleus through the famous gold foil experiment.
